F-Zero

System: SNES

Review

Game Type: 3D Racing
Hovercar racing set in the future. Race around a track, being careful not to hit the sides too much, or your craft may explode. Use jump ramps to clear gaps in the track.

Gameplay: 90/100
The controls are too tight to be realistic (what does a hovercar handle like, anyway?) but reality aside, the gameplay is very solid. No missiles or mines or anything of that sort but you'll be too busy negotiating tough turns to worry about that sort of thing. Lots and lots of tracks available - 3 classes with eight tracks each. Best records are saved for each track, and a time trial mode is available.

Graphics: 90/100
The graphics are showing their age a bit now but they still look decent. The track consists of a flat plane that is scaled and rotated according to your current position. The graphics are very colorful and the backgrounds are simplistic but beautiful.

Sound: 80/100
Not too fancy but the music and sound are pretty high quality. The alarm that goes off when your ship is badly damaged may drive you crazy if it goes too long.

Overall: /100
Time was when this was the most graphically amazing racing game in existence. That's certainly no longer the case, but F-Zero still has the excellent gameplay it needs to become a classic.
